| Denverite weather coverage | Denver-focused news coverage of local weather events | https://denverite.com/ |

**Note on reliability:** For actual historical measurements (not forecasts or averages), the NWS BOU page and Thornton Weather are the most reliable long-term. CPR and Denverite are useful for event narratives and citizen-reported totals. `timeanddate.com` and `world-weather.info` block automated fetches (HTTP 403) and should be used manually in a browser rather than via automated lookup.

### 2026-05-05–06 — Late-season hard freeze + snow

**Sources:**
- [CPR News: "A spring weather rollercoaster will hit the Front Range this week"](https://www.cpr.org/2026/05/04/winter-storm-colorado-weather-may/) (published 2026-05-04)
- [Denverite: "A winter storm will bring snow, a freeze warning and possible power outages to Denver"](https://denverite.com/2026/05/04/denver-weather-winter-storm-watch-may/) (published 2026-05-04)
- [CPR News: "Spring snowstorm creates slick roads, power outages across the Front Range"](https://www.cpr.org/2026/05/06/spring-snowstorm-slick-roads-power-outages/) (published 2026-05-06)
- [The Watchers: "Heavy, wet May snow disrupts Colorado's Front Range"](https://watchers.news/2026/05/06/heavy-wet-may-snow-disrupts-colorado-front-range-may-2026/) (published 2026-05-06)

**What happened:**
A late-season winter storm moved through the Front Range May 5–6. Temperatures dropped from the 70s into the low 30s. A Freeze Warning was issued with forecast lows of **22–28°F**. The NWS Denver office recorded 5.7 inches of snow at midnight; NWS forecast 5–8 inches along the I-25 corridor, up to 12 inches near the foothills. Scattered power outages across Jefferson and Boulder counties.

**Garden impact (assessed 2026-06-01):**

| Crop / location | Impact | Notes |
|-----------------|--------|-------|
| Strawberries (PS1, covered) | Likely flower damage | Row cover provides ~4–6°F protection; 22–28°F ambient = ~26–34°F under cover. Strawberry flowers are killed at ≤28°F. Slow growth as of 2026-06-01 is consistent with post-freeze recovery (~3.5 weeks post-event). Check for blackened flower centers — killed flowers will not set fruit. |
| Kale (PS2, covered) | Minimal | Kale is frost-hardy to ~10°F; covered plants likely fine. |
| Peach + cherry (backyard, uncovered) | Possible flower damage | Fruit trees were likely past bloom by early May. Monitor fruit set — any frost-killed blooms won't set fruit. |
| Other covered beds (FS2) | Minimal | Brussels sprouts not yet planted; onions frost-hardy. |
| FS1 (uncovered) | Low | Rosemary and onions; both frost-tolerant. |

**What to watch:**
- Strawberries: if no fruit sets this season on June-bearing plants, the flowers were killed. Plants should still be healthy and productive in 2027.
- Everbearing strawberries will push a second flush later in summer — this is less affected by a spring freeze.
